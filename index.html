Visualizing World Values

Discovery process 1


Most of the variables I took concern religion. The idea behind was to understand the evolution of religion, its values, and its impact on happiness.


- Is religion decreasing in the world?


1990-1994



Most of the countries with available data are highly religious (>60% of people answered they were religious).


2010-2014:



We observe a slight decrease of religion in countries such as United states but an increase or a stagnation in some countries like South Africa or Sweden. Thus, it doesn’t seem like religion is decreasing.


- Does “being religious” mean that you attend religious services often?


1990-1994:



2010-2014:



The more you are religious the more you attend religious services (of course). Countries are more located in the right part of the diagonal. Thus, for some countries being religious doesn’t mean you are consistent at attending religious services.


- Does religion provide happiness?


1990-1994:



2010-2014:



No correlation. Some countries are very religious but less happy (Romania), while Sweden is not religious at all and very happy.


The study allowed us to know that Religion is slightly decreasing in some countries, but the overall trend tell us that it doesn’t really decreases. Also, we cannot conclude on the effect of religion over happiness, and “being religious” is something different for different countries: for some you have to attend religious services often, while for others you don’t necessarily have to.
